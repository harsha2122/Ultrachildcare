Hand Therapy

Specialized hand therapy to restore function, strength, and precision grip following injury, surgery, or disease affecting the hands and wrists.

About Hand Therapy

Hand therapy is a specialized discipline combining occupational and physical therapy principles to address injuries, conditions, and post-operative needs of the hand and upper extremity. Our therapists are trained in advanced hand rehabilitation techniques.

Conditions We Treat

  • Post-Surgical Recovery: Carpal tunnel release, tendon repair, arthroscopy
  • Traumatic Injuries: Fractures, lacerations, burns, crush injuries
  • Arthritis: Osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis management
  • Repetitive Strain: Tendinitis, nerve compressions, occupational injuries

Treatment Focus Areas

  • Range of Motion Restoration
  • Strength and Endurance Training
  • Fine Motor Coordination
  • Grip and Pinch Strength
  • Functional Hand Tasks Training
  • Scar Tissue Management

Therapy Techniques

We employ evidence-based techniques including therapeutic exercises, manual therapy, modalities for pain management, adaptive equipment training, and work simulation activities. Each program is tailored to your specific condition and functional goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Timing depends on the type of surgery. Some cases begin therapy immediately, while others require waiting periods. Your surgeon will determine the appropriate timeline to ensure safe healing while preventing stiffness.

Results vary based on injury severity and individual healing. Most patients notice improvements within 2-3 weeks, with significant functional gains at 6-8 weeks. Consistent adherence to therapy and home exercises accelerates recovery.

Home exercise programs are critical to recovery. We provide detailed instructions for range-of-motion exercises, strengthening activities, and functional tasks. Consistent practice 2-3 times daily significantly enhances therapy outcomes.
