Physical Disability Therapy

Physical Disability Rehabilitation

Comprehensive therapy designed to maximize functional mobility and independence in children with physical disabilities. We focus on strength, balance, flexibility, and adaptive strategies.

Conditions Treated

  • Cerebral Palsy
  • Spina Bifida
  • Muscular Dystrophy
  • Limb Loss/Amputation
  • Joint contractures
  • Mobility disorders
  • Post-injury rehabilitation

Treatment Goals

Mobility: Walking, movement, transfers
Strength: Muscle power and endurance
Balance: Postural stability and coordination
Independence: Self-care and daily activities

FAQs

Yes, consistent physical therapy can improve mobility, strength, and independence even in children with significant disabilities.

We recommend assistive devices like braces, walkers, or mobility aids to enhance safety and independence based on individual needs.

Frequency varies based on severity. Typically 2-3 times weekly for optimal progress, combined with home exercises.
